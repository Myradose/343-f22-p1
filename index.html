Ultimate Self-Study Japanese Learning Guide

What is this guide?

This is a compilation of everything I wish I knew when I tried to learn Japanese a while back. Many of the resources in this guide are very new and won’t be included in similar guides online. One of the best aspects of this guide is that just about every resource shown in this guide is centered around the Genki textbooks so there isn’t any study overlap.

In this guide there are resources included for multiple learning styles ranging from very textual to very visual. In my personal experience, the more visual/classroom-like experience you go, the better your understanding of application will be, but I wouldn’t go overboard watching every supplementary visual material I offer here.

Does anything cost money?

Technically yes, but most are free. You can buy the Genki textbooks or be a sneaky pirate and get them for free. I’m definitely not going to stop you. I’ve heard that there’s a website called libgen that has amazing 100% discounts on pdfs of the Genki textbooks, Genki workbooks and any other academic textbook you may need but I wouldn’t know for sure because I definitely haven’t used it before. ;)

How should I pace myself?

Ultimately that is up to you since this is self-study, however, I HIGHLY recommend you do realkana and the Anki decks every day if possible. Once you start lesson 2 or 3 (depending on whether you want to stay ahead), I would start working through WaniKani. While doing WaniKani, start writing the corresponding Ringotan kanji. It is always okay to get ahead (I even recommend it) on Anki vocabulary/kanji since it makes future lessons and videos easier, but if you want to do everything side by side, set your deck to have no new cards once you finish whatever lesson you are on so you are at least reviewing and putting your learned cards in long term memory every day.
