Ultimate Self-Study Japanese Learning Guide

Getting ready

To start your Japanese journey, there are a few resources that are absolutely necessary.

  • Genki 1 textbook (digital or physical)
  • A laptop and/or smartphone
  • Anki
  • WaniKani subscription

If you can't afford/don't want to pay for a WaniKani subscription, you can still progress through this guide since it is uncorrelated with the genki curriculum. That being said, I HIGHLY reccomend getting a WaniKani subscription if you can and I think it is important enough to be considered a "requirement".

Anki

Now we need to setup Anki.

If you don’t know what Anki is, it is a flashcard software designed to ingrain all learned vocabulary/flashcards into long term memory. It does this using SRS. (spaced repetition software) This basically means that stuff you know will be shown less and stuff you don’t know will be shown more. I HIGHLY recommend you do your daily cards for whatever decks you choose every day. Unless you are doing many decks at once this should only take you max 30-60 minutes. If you find it is taking longer or you can’t allocate 30-60 minutes out of your day, adjust the settings so that you are shown fewer new cards each day.

There are two decks that we will use in this guide.

Ringotan

While not required, if you plan on doing writing practice, I highly recommend getting Ringotan. This is an amazing app that can teach you how to write hiragana, katakana, and the Genki kanji in proper stroke order. It also uses SRS similar to Anki to facilitate everything you learn into your long term memory. It will eventually be a paid app but is completely free forever for anyone who downloads it now while it’s still in development.
